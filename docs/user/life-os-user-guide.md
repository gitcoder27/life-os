# Life OS User Guide

## What Life OS Is

Life OS is a single-user personal operating system for professionals who want one place to run their day, maintain consistency, and stay aligned across work and life.

It combines:

- daily planning and execution
- capture and triage
- habits and routines
- health basics
- expense tracking and recurring bills
- goals and planning
- daily, weekly, and monthly reviews
- notifications and reminders

The core idea is simple:

```text
Capture -> Triage -> Execute -> Track -> Review -> Reset -> Repeat
```

This is not just a dashboard and not just a to-do app. It is designed to help you operate your life in cycles:

- decide what matters
- do the right work
- log the basics that affect consistency
- review what happened
- turn that review into the next plan

## What Life OS Helps You Do

Life OS helps you answer the questions that matter every day:

- What matters today?
- What should I do next?
- What have I captured but not yet decided?
- Am I staying consistent with habits and routines?
- How am I doing on health basics and spending?
- Am I still aligned with my larger goals?
- What should tomorrow, next week, or next month focus on?

## What Life OS Is Not

Life OS is intentionally focused. In its current form, it is:

- a single-user web app, not a team collaboration tool
- manual-input first, not an integration-heavy automation platform
- a lightweight finance tracker, not full accounting software
- a lightweight health tracker, not a full coaching or nutrition platform
- a planning system, not a complex project-management suite
- a reflection tool with structured reviews, not an AI autopilot

That matters because the product works best when used as a clear operating system, not as an all-in-one replacement for every specialist app.

## The Best Mental Model

If you remember only one thing, remember this page model:

- **Home** is where you decide.
- **Inbox** is where you triage.
- **Today** is where you execute.
- **Habits, Health, and Finance** are where you keep the basics current.
- **Goals** are where direction is defined.
- **Reviews** are where cycles are closed and the next cycle is seeded.
- **Notifications** are where unresolved items come back to you.
- **Settings** are where the system rules are tuned to your life.

## How the App Fits Together

```text
Sign in
  -> Optional starter setup
  -> Home

Quick Capture
  -> Inbox
  -> Decide whether an item belongs on Today, later, or just as reference

Home
  -> Sends you to Today, Habits, Health, Finance, Inbox, Reviews

Today
  -> Holds the top 3 priorities and today's task lane

Habits / Health / Finance
  -> Feed the score, momentum, guidance, and review system

Goals
  -> Link into priorities, habits, tasks, and planning cycles

Daily Review
  -> Resolves unfinished work and seeds tomorrow's top 3 priorities

Weekly Review
  -> Seeds next week's priorities and a focus habit

Monthly Review
  -> Seeds next month's theme and top outcomes

Notifications
  -> Pull you back to the page that needs attention
```

## First-Time User Journey

### 1. Sign in

You enter through a standard email-and-password sign-in page.

### 2. Optional starter setup

If onboarding is incomplete, Life OS can guide you through a starter setup wizard. This is optional, not mandatory.

The setup wizard helps you define:

- your display name
- life priorities
- your first top goals
- morning and evening routines
- daily habits
- water target
- expense categories
- daily review time
- week start day

If you skip this, you can still configure everything later from the rest of the app.

### 3. Review settings early

Before using the system seriously, it is worth checking:

- timezone
- currency
- week start day
- daily water target
- daily review window
- notification preferences

These settings affect how dates, review windows, and reminders behave.

### 4. Build just enough structure

A good starting setup is:

- 3 active goals
- 1 morning routine
- 1 evening routine
- 3 to 5 habits
- a realistic water target
- a small set of expense categories

Life OS works best when the structure is meaningful but not overloaded.

## Page-by-Page Guide

### Home

**Purpose**

Home is the command center. It gives you awareness, not deep control.

**What you see**

- daily score and score label
- weekly momentum
- strong-day streak
- why your score is lower than potential
- recovery guidance and recommendations
- today's open priorities and open tasks
- routines summary
- health snapshot
- inbox preview
- finance snapshot
- attention items that link directly to the right page

**How to use it well**

- Start here to understand the day.
- Do not try to work from here for long.
- Use it to choose the next page, then move there quickly.

**Best interpretation**

- If Home feels busy, that usually means you should go to `Today`, `Inbox`, or a review, not stay on Home.

### Inbox

**Purpose**

Inbox is the triage layer. Everything captured should be decided here before it becomes active work.

**What lands here**

- captured tasks
- captured notes
- captured reminders

**What you can do**

- filter the queue by tasks, notes, or reminders
- inspect one item at a time
- send an item to `Today`
- schedule it for a future date
- link it to a goal
- convert a task into a note
- archive it

**How to use it well**

- Treat Inbox as a decision point, not a storage dump.
- Triage regularly so capture stays low-friction.
- Only promote items to `Today` if they truly belong in the active day.

**Important distinction**

- `Inbox` is for undecided work.
- `Today` is for committed work.

### Today

**Purpose**

Today is the execution workspace. This is where the day is worked.

**What you can do**

- define and reorder the top 3 priorities
- link priorities to goals
- work through today's task lane
- complete, reopen, drop, carry forward, or reschedule tasks
- view quick-capture notes that belong to today
- see timed tasks as time blocks
- see a compact meals-and-training context for the day

**How to use it well**

- Keep the top 3 priorities honest and specific.
- Use drag-and-drop to rank what matters before you start working.
- Clear, move, or drop every task that does not belong in today anymore.
- Keep `Today` small enough that it stays usable.

**What makes this page strong**

- It is opinionated. It pushes you toward fewer priorities and explicit task decisions instead of passive backlog accumulation.

### Habits

**Purpose**

Habits is the consistency layer.

**What you can do**

- complete habits due today with one tap
- see streaks
- see risk states such as drifting or at risk
- complete morning and evening routines
- see a weekly challenge or focus habit
- see a 7-day consistency view
- create, edit, schedule, and archive habits
- create, edit, and archive routines
- link habits to goals

**How to use it well**

- Keep habits meaningful, not trivial.
- Use routines for step-by-step checklists and habits for repeat behaviors.
- Review risk badges early, especially when a streak matters.
- If a habit supports a goal, link it so the system can connect direction to execution.

### Health

**Purpose**

Health tracks a small number of high-signal daily inputs.

**What you can do**

- log water quickly
- review today's water progress against target
- log meals from templates or freeform
- create and manage meal templates
- mark workout status as completed, rest day, or missed
- log and review body-weight entries
- see a 7-day snapshot for water, workouts, and weight trend

**How to use it well**

- Keep logging simple and same-day.
- Use meal templates for recurring meals to reduce friction.
- Treat Health as a consistency surface, not as a deep analytics tool.

**Important note**

- Health contributes to score and review quality, so same-day logging is more valuable than late cleanup.

### Finance

**Purpose**

Finance gives you lightweight spending visibility without turning Life OS into a full finance app.

**What you can do**

- log expenses quickly
- assign categories
- edit and delete recent expenses
- manage expense categories
- view current month spend
- see top spend category
- view upcoming bills
- create, edit, pause, resume, and archive recurring bills

**How to use it well**

- Log spend the day it happens.
- Keep categories small and practical.
- Use recurring bills for known repeat obligations, not for every financial detail.

**Best use case**

- This page is best for awareness and discipline, not budgeting complexity.

### Goals

**Purpose**

Goals is the direction layer. It connects longer-horizon outcomes to current work.

**What you can do**

- create and manage goals by life domain
- filter goals by domain and status
- define monthly focus and top monthly outcomes
- define weekly priorities
- open a goal detail panel
- manage milestones for a goal
- see goal progress, health, momentum, and next best action
- see linked priorities, linked tasks, and linked habits
- reactivate inactive goals

**How to use it well**

- Keep active goals limited.
- Use milestones to make goals operational.
- Link daily and weekly work to goals when it matters.
- Use the goal detail panel to understand whether a goal is on track, drifting, stalled, or achieved.

**How it connects**

- Goals can be linked to daily priorities, tasks, habits, weekly priorities, and monthly outcomes.
- The page is not just a list of aspirations; it is a planning surface.

### Reviews

**Purpose**

Reviews are the reset mechanism. This is where the system closes the loop.

Life OS supports three review cadences:

- **Daily review**: close the day and seed tomorrow
- **Weekly review**: understand the pattern and set next-week direction
- **Monthly review**: reset the larger system and simplify the next month

**Daily review**

The daily review is operational, not philosophical.

You do three things:

- review the system-generated summary for the day
- decide what to do with unfinished tasks: carry forward, drop, or reschedule
- enter tomorrow's top 3 priorities

You also capture a short reflection:

- biggest win
- main friction
- energy rating
- optional note

**Weekly review**

The weekly review is where pattern recognition starts.

It helps you reflect on:

- biggest win
- biggest miss
- main lesson
- what to keep
- what to improve

It also lets you choose:

- next-week priorities
- one focus habit for the coming week

That focus habit becomes the weekly challenge surfaced elsewhere in the app.

**Monthly review**

The monthly review is the system reset.

It helps you define:

- your overall verdict on the month
- biggest win
- biggest leak
- next-month theme
- three key outcomes for next month
- what to simplify

**Important review rules**

- Reviews are controlled by submission windows.
- Daily reviews open in the evening and close the next morning, based on your settings.
- Weekly reviews are for the previous week during the current week.
- Monthly reviews are for the previous month during the current month.
- Once a review is submitted, it becomes locked and read-only.

**How to use reviews well**

- Do not skip daily review if you want the system to stay coherent.
- Use weekly review to decide, not to journal endlessly.
- Use monthly review to simplify the next month, not to create too many ambitions.

### Review History

**Purpose**

Review History is the learning layer. It turns separate reviews into a real archive.

**What you can do**

- browse daily, weekly, and monthly reviews in one place
- filter by cadence
- filter by time range
- search past reflections
- see recurring friction tags
- view weekly and monthly trends
- compare current and previous weekly or monthly periods
- reopen the underlying review entry

**How to use it well**

- Use it when you want pattern visibility, not just one review screen.
- Search for repeated issues such as overcommitment, low energy, or distraction.
- Review comparison sections when you want evidence of improvement or drift.

### Notifications

**Purpose**

Notifications are the action center for things that need attention.

**What you can do**

- filter by all, needs action, or read
- see notifications grouped by urgency
- open the relevant page directly
- mark items as read
- snooze them for one hour, tonight, or tomorrow
- dismiss them entirely

**What kinds of notifications exist**

- review reminders
- finance reminders
- health nudges
- habit alerts
- routine prompts

**How to use it well**

- Treat notifications as a re-entry surface, not a place to live.
- Snooze when timing is wrong; dismiss when the issue is truly resolved.

### Settings

**Purpose**

Settings controls the rules of the system.

**What you can change**

- display name
- timezone
- currency
- week start day
- daily water target
- daily review start and end time
- notification behavior by category and severity

**How to use it well**

- Set timezone correctly before relying on reviews and reminders.
- Use realistic targets. A too-ambitious target weakens the system.
- Tune notifications so they remain useful, not noisy.

### Quick Capture

**Purpose**

Quick Capture is the fastest entry point in the entire app. It exists so you can log or capture without breaking flow.

**How to open it**

- click `Quick capture`
- use `Ctrl+K` or `Cmd+K`

**What you can capture**

- task
- expense
- water
- meal
- workout
- weight
- note
- reminder

**How it behaves**

- captured tasks go to `Inbox` first
- notes and reminders also go to `Inbox`
- reminders preserve their reminder date
- recurring reminders can be created from capture
- health and finance entries log directly into their own systems

**How to use it well**

- Capture fast now, organize later.
- Do not interrupt work to fully structure an item unless necessary.

## Score, Momentum, and Guidance

Life OS includes a transparent scoring system so the user can see whether the day is aligned, not just busy.

### Daily score

The daily score is a `0-100` score built from five buckets:

- plan and priorities
- routines and habits
- health basics
- finance and admin
- review and reset

Only applicable buckets count. If there was no workout planned and no bill due, those parts do not unfairly drag down the score.

### Score labels

- `85-100`: Strong Day
- `70-84`: Solid Day
- `55-69`: Recovering Day
- `Below 55`: Off-Track Day

### Weekly momentum

Weekly momentum rolls up recent finalized daily scores and adds a bonus for completing the weekly review.

### Guidance

Home uses this data to generate:

- recovery guidance
- weekly challenge reminders
- recommended next actions
- attention items

That makes the system active rather than passive.

## Recommended Workflows

### Workflow 1: Daily operating loop

Use this when Life OS is your main daily control system.

1. Start on `Home` to see score, guidance, and attention items.
2. Move to `Today` and set or confirm the top 3 priorities.
3. Check `Habits` if routines or due habits need to be cleared early.
4. Log water, meals, workout, and spend as they happen in `Health` and `Finance`, or use Quick Capture.
5. Capture anything that should not interrupt you with `Quick Capture`.
6. Triage `Inbox` so only real work reaches `Today`.
7. End with `Daily review` to resolve unfinished work and seed tomorrow.

### Workflow 2: Capture and triage loop

Use this when you are moving quickly and do not want to lose ideas or obligations.

1. Open `Quick Capture`.
2. Capture the item with minimal effort.
3. Visit `Inbox` later.
4. Decide whether the item should:
   - go to today
   - be scheduled later
   - be linked to a goal
   - stay as a note
   - be archived

This keeps capture friction low and execution space clean.

### Workflow 3: Goal-driven planning loop

Use this when you want daily work to support a real direction.

1. Define active goals in `Goals`.
2. Add milestones to each meaningful goal.
3. Set monthly focus and weekly priorities.
4. Link habits and daily priorities to goals where relevant.
5. Use the goal detail panel to monitor linked work, momentum, and next best action.

### Workflow 4: Weekly reset loop

Use this once each week.

1. Open the weekly review during its submission window.
2. Reflect on wins, misses, and lessons.
3. Decide what to keep and what to improve.
4. Choose one focus habit for the coming week.
5. Let the review seed next-week priorities.
6. Return to `Goals` and `Today` with a clearer direction.

### Workflow 5: Monthly reset loop

Use this at the start of each month.

1. Open the monthly review during the current month for the previous month.
2. Decide what the month really meant.
3. Identify the biggest leak in time, energy, or money.
4. Define next month's theme.
5. Define the three most important outcomes.
6. Use `Goals` to translate that into weekly and daily execution.

## How to Use Life OS Effectively

### Keep the roles of pages clear

- Use `Home` for awareness.
- Use `Inbox` for triage.
- Use `Today` for execution.

Confusing those roles will make the system feel heavier than it is.

### Keep inputs meaningful

The system becomes stronger when you track:

- real priorities
- real habits
- meaningful meals
- real expenses

Avoid logging for the sake of logging.

### Link only what matters

Not every task needs a goal link. Use links for the items that truly support a goal. This keeps goal tracking meaningful.

### Close loops aggressively

The app is designed around closure:

- unfinished tasks should be carried forward, dropped, or rescheduled
- reviews should turn reflection into next steps
- notifications should be opened, snoozed, or dismissed

### Protect simplicity

Life OS works best when:

- active goals are limited
- habits are few but important
- routines are practical
- Today is not overloaded
- Inbox is triaged regularly

## Current Scope and Practical Limits

For a new user, these are useful expectations to keep in mind:

- The app is intentionally manual and lightweight.
- It does not replace your calendar or bank.
- It is designed for one user, not shared planning.
- Reviews currently support full submission, not ongoing draft save.
- Goals are more operational than a simple list, but still lighter than a full project-management system.
- Life admin exists only lightly through tasks, reminders, and recurring items, not as a dedicated module.

## Short Version

Life OS is best understood as a professional personal operating system built around one disciplined loop:

1. capture without friction
2. triage before committing
3. execute from a focused daily workspace
4. keep habits, health, and finance current
5. review the cycle and seed the next one

If you use it that way, the pages stop feeling like separate modules and start working like one connected system.
