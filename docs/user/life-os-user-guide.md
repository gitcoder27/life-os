# Life OS User Guide

## What Life OS Is

Life OS is a single-user personal operating system for running your day with more intention and less drift.

It combines:

- daily planning and execution
- inbox triage and capture
- habits and routines
- health basics and meal planning
- expense tracking, bills, and monthly money planning
- goals, hierarchy, and alignment
- daily, weekly, and monthly reviews
- notifications and reminders

The core loop is still simple:

```text
Capture -> Triage -> Execute -> Track -> Review -> Reset -> Repeat
```

Life OS works best when you use it as an operating system for decisions and follow-through, not as a place to hoard information.

## What Life OS Helps You Do

Life OS helps you answer:

- What matters today?
- What needs triage before it becomes active work?
- What should I do now, later today, or move out of today?
- Am I staying consistent with habits, routines, hydration, meals, and workouts?
- Am I keeping spending, bills, and monthly money targets under control?
- Are my daily priorities connected to larger goals?
- What should next week or next month focus on?

## What Life OS Is Not

In its current form, Life OS is:

- a single-user web app, not a team workspace
- manual-entry first, not an automation-heavy platform
- a lightweight finance and planning system, not accounting software
- a focused health tracker, not a full coaching platform
- a goal and review system, not a complex project suite

That focus is intentional. The product is strongest when you keep it lean and current.

## Best Mental Model

If you remember only one model, use this:

- **Home** decides what deserves attention.
- **Inbox** decides what captured items mean.
- **Today** runs the day in either execute mode or plan mode.
- **Habits** keeps your consistency system alive.
- **Health** tracks your body basics and meal plan.
- **Finance** keeps spending, bills, and monthly pacing visible.
- **Goals** connects longer-term direction to today, this week, and this month.
- **Reviews** closes loops and seeds the next cycle.
- **Settings** controls the system rules.
- **Quick Capture** lets you add things without breaking flow.
- **Notifications** pull you back only when something needs attention.

## How the App Fits Together

```text
Sign in
  -> Optional starter setup
  -> Home

Quick Capture
  -> Tasks / notes / reminders go to Inbox
  -> Expenses and health logs are recorded immediately

Inbox
  -> Decide what belongs in Today, later, or nowhere

Today
  -> Execute work now
  -> Or switch to Plan mode and shape the day with blocks

Habits / Health / Finance
  -> Keep the supporting systems current

Goals
  -> Align today, this week, and this month with larger outcomes

Reviews
  -> Close the current cycle
  -> Seed tomorrow, next week, or next month

Review History
  -> Study patterns across past reviews

Notifications
  -> Re-enter the right screen when something needs action
```

## First-Time User Journey

### 1. Sign in

You enter through a standard email-and-password sign-in page.

### 2. Optional starter setup

If onboarding is still incomplete, Life OS offers an optional starter setup wizard.

It helps you define:

- display name
- life priorities
- your first starter goals
- morning and evening routines
- daily habits
- water target
- expense categories
- daily review time
- week start day

If you leave some sections blank, Life OS fills in sensible starter defaults.

### 3. Review settings early

Before you rely on the system, check:

- timezone
- currency
- week start day
- daily water target
- daily review window
- notification behavior

These settings directly affect dates, review windows, reminders, and pacing.

### 4. Start small

A strong starting setup is:

- up to 3 active goals
- 1 morning routine
- 1 evening routine
- 3 to 5 habits
- a realistic water target
- a small set of expense categories

## Page-by-Page Guide

### Home

**Purpose**

Home is your command center. It is for awareness and direction, not for staying parked all day.

**What you see**

- daily score and score label
- weekly momentum and strong-day streak
- a focus prompt for the current phase of the day
- recovery and guidance recommendations
- at-risk items and attention links
- top priorities and open-task context
- routines, habits, health, and finance summaries
- inbox preview

**How to use it well**

- Start here when you want a fast read on the day.
- Use the attention items to jump straight to the right page.
- If Home feels busy, that is usually a signal to move into `Inbox`, `Today`, or a review.

### Inbox

**Purpose**

Inbox is the triage layer for captured tasks, notes, and reminders.

**What you can do**

- filter by all, tasks, notes, or reminders
- inspect one item in a side panel
- edit title and notes inline
- move an item to today
- schedule it for a future date
- link it to a goal
- convert items between note and reminder states
- archive items
- select multiple items for bulk schedule or archive actions
- use workflow templates to drop reusable task bundles into Inbox

**Important distinctions**

- `Inbox` is for undecided items.
- `Today` is for committed work.
- Quick-captured tasks, notes, and reminders land here first by design.

**How to use it well**

- Triage regularly instead of letting Inbox become storage.
- Use templates for repeated checklists or recurring workflows.
- Link items to goals only when the link is genuinely useful for alignment.

### Today

**Purpose**

Today is the daily operating workspace. It has two modes:

- **Execute**: run the day
- **Plan**: shape the day with planner blocks

**What you can do in Execute mode**

- define, reorder, complete, or remove up to 3 top priorities
- link priorities to goals
- work through tasks grouped into `Now`, `Later today`, and `Unplanned`
- complete, reopen, drop, move to tomorrow, or reschedule tasks
- review overdue work in the recovery tray
- keep day notes visible
- see daily essentials like routines, habits, and health context

**What you can do in Plan mode**

- switch to another date and plan future days
- create, edit, reorder, duplicate, split, and nudge planner blocks
- assign unplanned tasks into blocks with drag and drop
- adjust visible planner hours
- clean up drifted blocks and move leftover work forward

**Important behavior**

- Past planner dates can be viewed but are not meant for active editing.
- If you do not create planner blocks, Life OS still helps you run the day by surfacing natural `Now` tasks.

**How to use it well**

- Keep top priorities honest and limited.
- Use Execute mode when you need momentum.
- Use Plan mode when the day needs structure, not just a task list.

### Habits

**Purpose**

Habits is the consistency system.

**What you can do**

- check off habits due today
- complete routine items for active routines
- see streaks, momentum, and consistency bars
- see weekly challenge or focus-habit signals
- create, edit, archive, and restore habits
- create, edit, reorder, archive, and restore routines
- pause habits temporarily for breaks or vacation windows
- link habits to goals

**How to use it well**

- Use habits for repeat behaviors and routines for step-by-step sequences.
- Pause habits when life changes instead of silently abandoning them.
- Keep the system small enough that completion means something.

### Health

**Purpose**

Health tracks daily body basics with low friction.

**What you can do**

- see a health score and current focus prompt
- log water with one tap
- log meals from today's plan, from a template, or freeform
- update workout status as completed, rest day, or missed
- log weight
- review today's activity timeline
- edit or delete same-day water, meal, and weight entries
- see 7-day patterns for hydration, meals, workouts, and weight trend
- manage a lightweight library of meal templates

**How to use it well**

- Keep logging same-day whenever possible.
- Use the quick-action rail for speed.
- Treat Health as a consistency surface, not a deep analytics suite.

### Health > Meals

**Purpose**

Meals is the weekly meal-planning workspace under Health.

**What you can do**

- plan breakfast, lunch, dinner, and snacks across the week
- assign saved recipes or meal templates to slots
- edit planned servings and notes
- see which planned meals have already been logged
- manage prep sessions
- build and edit a grocery list
- keep week notes
- browse and edit recipe or meal-template details

**How to use it well**

- Use this page for weekly planning.
- Use `Health` for same-day execution and logging.
- Keep your grocery list and prep sessions tied to the week you are actually running.

### Finance

**Purpose**

Finance keeps spending, bills, and monthly money pacing visible without turning the app into full bookkeeping.

**What you can do**

- move between months
- log expenses quickly
- edit, delete, and recategorize recent expenses
- filter recent activity by all, today, uncategorized, or recurring
- see bills that are overdue, due today, or due this week
- mark bills paid, reschedule them, log an expense from them, or drop them
- define a monthly spending plan
- track fixed obligations, flexible target, planned income, large expenses, and category watch limits
- review category totals and pending bills
- connect finance insight panels to money-related goals
- manage categories
- create, edit, pause, resume, and archive recurring bills

**How to use it well**

- Log spending as it happens.
- Use the monthly plan to keep pace visible before you drift.
- Use category watches and recurring bills for the few things that actually need management.

### Goals

**Purpose**

Goals is the direction layer. It connects daily execution to weekly, monthly, and longer-horizon planning.

**What you can do in Overview mode**

- create and manage goals by domain
- filter by domain, planning layer, and status
- view goals grouped by domain
- open a goal detail inspector
- manage milestones
- edit weekly priorities
- edit monthly theme and top outcomes
- reactivate inactive goals

**What you can do in Plan mode**

- work with goal hierarchy in outline or graph form
- break a goal down into sub-goals
- inspect supporting goals and linked habits
- see whether a goal is represented in today, this week, or this month
- add a goal's next best action into today's priorities when appropriate
- manage planning alignment across today, week, and month

**How to use it well**

- Keep active goals limited.
- Use milestones and sub-goals to make goals operational.
- Use weekly priorities and monthly outcomes as the bridge between long-term goals and daily work.

### Reviews

**Purpose**

Reviews close the loop and seed the next cycle.

Life OS supports three review cadences:

- **Daily**
- **Weekly**
- **Monthly**

**Daily review**

The daily review is short and operational. It lets you:

- review a generated summary of the day
- capture biggest win, main friction, energy, and an optional note
- decide what to do with unresolved tasks: carry, drop, or reschedule
- define tomorrow's 3 priorities

If a daily review is already submitted, you can still edit it until that review window closes.

**Weekly review**

The weekly review is pattern-focused. It includes:

- a generated summary panel
- required reflection prompts
- a focus habit selector for next week

Submitting the weekly review seeds next-week priorities and the weekly challenge habit.

**Monthly review**

The monthly review resets the larger system. It includes:

- a generated summary panel
- required reflection prompts
- next-month theme and outcome planning

Submitting the monthly review seeds next-month theme and outcomes.

**Important review rules**

- Review windows are controlled by your timezone and settings.
- Daily review timing depends on your configured daily review window.
- Weekly and monthly reviews can show window guidance when you are too early, too late, or looking at the wrong period.
- Weekly and monthly reviews become locked after submission.

### Review History

**Purpose**

Review History turns separate reviews into a usable archive.

**What you can do**

- browse daily, weekly, and monthly review entries together
- filter by cadence
- filter by time range
- search reflections
- see total review counts and recurring friction tags
- load older entries
- view weekly and monthly trend cards
- compare current and previous weekly or monthly periods
- reopen the original review screen

**How to use it well**

- Use it for pattern recognition, not just nostalgia.
- Search for repeated friction themes.
- Check comparisons when you want evidence that the system is improving or slipping.

### Notifications

**Purpose**

Notifications are a header panel that helps you re-enter the right place quickly.

**What you can do**

- open the notification center from the bell icon in the top header
- filter by all, needs action, or read
- review notifications grouped by severity
- open the related app surface directly
- mark notifications as read
- snooze for 1 hour, tonight, or tomorrow
- dismiss individual notifications or clear all

**What kinds of notifications exist**

- tasks
- inbox recovery and inbox-zero updates
- review reminders
- finance alerts
- health nudges
- habit alerts
- routine prompts

**Important note**

- Notifications are not a standalone page anymore.
- Use them as a re-entry surface, not as a place to work from.

### Settings

**Purpose**

Settings controls the rules and structure behind the app.

**What you can change**

- display name
- timezone
- currency
- week start day
- daily water target
- daily review start and end times
- goal domains
- goal planning layers or horizons
- notification preferences by category, severity, and repeat behavior
- current session logout

**How to use it well**

- Set timezone correctly before trusting review windows or reminders.
- Keep goal domains and planning layers stable unless your planning system has genuinely changed.
- Tune notifications so they stay useful instead of turning into wallpaper.

### Quick Capture

**Purpose**

Quick Capture is the fastest way to add something without leaving your current context.

**How to open it**

- use the sidebar button
- use the header `Capture` button
- press `Ctrl+K` or `Cmd+K`

**What you can capture**

- task
- note
- reminder
- expense
- water
- meal
- workout
- weight

**Important behavior**

- Tasks, notes, and reminders are captured into `Inbox` for later triage.
- Expenses and health entries are logged immediately.
- Reminders can include recurrence.

**How to use it well**

- Use Quick Capture for speed, not for perfect organization.
- Triage captured work later in `Inbox`.

## Recommended Daily Rhythm

### Start of day

- Check `Home` for the overall picture.
- Go to `Today` and confirm your 3 priorities.
- If the day is crowded, switch to `Today` plan mode and shape blocks.

### During the day

- Use Quick Capture instead of breaking focus.
- Triage Inbox before it piles up.
- Keep Health and Finance current on the same day.

### End of day

- Clear or consciously move unresolved tasks.
- Complete the daily review.
- Seed tomorrow before logging off.

## Final Advice

Life OS gets stronger when it stays current.

It does not need perfect data. It needs honest data, entered at the right time, in the right place:

- capture fast
- triage regularly
- keep today small
- log the basics
- review on schedule
- let goals shape priorities instead of sitting apart from them
